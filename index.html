Approaches 2–5

From a published framework to a daily habit

The Lens makes the Map legible. These four make it lived — meeting staff inside the platforms and routines they already use, and giving the institution a way to see it working.

02

Identity-matched pathways, built once and routed automatically

Personalised learning journeys · My Learning

Nobody should hunt for their development. By mapping the four leadership identities onto the platform's audience groups, each colleague signs in to a pathway already shaped to their level — a technician sees the Specialist view of a behaviour, a Head of School the University view of the same one. Content is authored once in Rise 360, held centrally, and surfaced to the right audience, so an update to a behaviour statement reaches every pathway at once.

ScalabilityLearning design InnovativeAmbitious
Why I can deliver this: I build identity-aware, branching pathways in Rise 360 and Storyline today, and maintain content centrally so a single edit updates everywhere it is used.
03

Behaviour-in-the-flow: small prompts where the work actually happens

Practical, day-to-day application

Leadership behaviour is built in moments, not modules. Short, opt-in nudges sit inside the tools staff already use — a one-line reflection before a difficult conversation, a coaching question ahead of a one-to-one, a 90-second micro-lesson when a relevant task appears. Each is tagged to a Map behaviour, so a small everyday action is quietly connected to the bigger picture without adding to anyone's workload.

Engagement & adoptionDay-to-day CollaborativeEnterprising
Why I can deliver this: my asynchronous induction and micro-content already meet staff and students inside live modules rather than asking them to come to a course.
04

A recognition wall where staff show the Map in action

Engagement, belonging & storytelling

The Map says leadership is shown, not held by title. A lightweight recognition space lets colleagues log a real moment — “a teammate had a brave, honest conversation” — and tag it to a behaviour. It makes the abstract concrete, spreads good practice peer-to-peer, and builds belonging by celebrating quiet, everyday leadership that usually goes unseen. Stories become a living library the Lens and pathways can draw on.

Engagement & adoptionCulture InclusiveCollaborative
Why I can deliver this: through the CATE Generative AI group I run participation spaces that get cautious colleagues contributing, not just consuming.
05

A diagnostic and dashboard that show the Map is working

Data, evaluation & continuous improvement

A short self-assessment lets any colleague locate themselves across the behaviours and choose two to grow — turning the Map into a personal starting point. Aggregated and anonymised, the same data gives OD&L a heat-map of confidence and engagement across job families, so investment goes where it is needed and the offer keeps improving. Evaluation is designed in from day one, not bolted on.

Designed against the brief

Every approach earns its place on four tests

None of the five is here to look clever. Each was kept only if it held up against the things this brief actually asks of a digital learning offer.

Learning design & delivery

Grounded in the Map's real behaviour statements and built in tools that track completion honestly.

Engagement & adoption

Opt-in, low-friction, peer-driven — pull, not push, so people choose to take part.

Day-to-day application

Behaviour met in the flow of real work, not parked in a course nobody returns to.

Scale across difference

One build, routed to four identities and every job family — and measured as it lands.

How this would run in My Learning

Start small, learn fast, then scale with confidence

The prototype would not need a new platform. It could begin as a focused My Learning pilot, using the same content model shown here: role identity, behaviour choice, practical prompt, reflection, and measured follow-up.

01

Pilot with one staff group

Run the Lens and two short pathways with a mixed group of professional services, academic and technical colleagues to test clarity, usefulness and confidence change.

02

Publish as a self-directed pathway

Package the activities in My Learning as short modules, job aids and reflection prompts, with optional nudges that point people to the next relevant behaviour.

03

Measure, improve and extend

Use completion, confidence checks and anonymised behaviour data to improve the offer before scaling across roles, services and future OD&L initiatives.

Accessible and inclusive by design: every activity would be keyboard-friendly, screen-reader labelled, written in plain language, checked against WCAG principles, and available as both an interactive experience and a static job aid for colleagues who need or prefer a lower-bandwidth route.

One thread runs through all five

Every approach is designed to lower the barrier, not raise the bar. The colleague who is unsure about new technology is exactly who these are built for — the Lens does the translating, the nudges arrive already in plain language, and the recognition wall asks for a sentence, not a submission. That is the Map's own Innovation behaviour, “experiments with new ideas and new technology,” made safe to try.

It is also the work I already do: taking expert frameworks and emerging tools and making them usable for people whose confidence and starting points vary enormously. I would be glad to build any one of these as a working pilot.
